Advanced Wireless and Electromagnetic Systems Research Lab (AWARE)

We explore applied and computational electromagnetics, AI applications, RF/microwave engineering, antennas, metamaterials, and electromagnetic compatibility. Our group solves fundamental and applied problems with real-world impact.

Meet the Group Explore Research

Research Mission

The AWARE Lab advances the science and engineering of electromagnetics, antennas, RF systems, and physics-informed machine learning. Our mission is to develop computationally efficient, physically grounded models and innovative electromagnetic technologies that enable next-generation wireless communication, terahertz systems, and intelligent sensing platforms.

We integrate fundamental electromagnetic theory with data-driven approaches to create scalable solutions for antenna optimization, waveguide analysis, wireless power transfer, and emerging high-frequency devices.

What We Do

AWARE Lab conducts research at the intersection of computational electromagnetics, antenna engineering, RF systems, and physics-informed machine learning. Our work spans theoretical modeling, numerical simulation, experimental validation, and real-world wireless system deployment.

Research Areas

Physics-Informed AI

Data-free and physics-embedded neural networks for scientific computing and surrogate modeling.

Antennas & RF Systems

Advanced antenna design, beamforming architectures, and electromagnetic optimization techniques.

Terahertz Devices

Modeling of plasma-wave devices and terahertz waveguide and amplifier systems.

Wireless Power Transfer

Innovative RF power telemetry, beam steering, and wearable energy harvesting systems.
