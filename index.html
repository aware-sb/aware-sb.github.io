Research Mission

The Applied Wireless and Antenna Research (AWARE) Lab advances the science and engineering of electromagnetics, antennas, RF systems, and physics-informed machine learning. Our mission is to develop computationally efficient, physically grounded models and innovative electromagnetic technologies that enable next-generation wireless communication, terahertz systems, and intelligent sensing platforms.

We integrate fundamental electromagnetic theory with data-driven approaches to create scalable solutions for antenna optimization, waveguide analysis, wireless power transfer, and emerging high-frequency devices.

Research Areas

Physics-Informed AI

Data-free and physics-embedded neural networks for scientific computing and surrogate modeling.

Antennas & RF Systems

Advanced antenna design, beamforming architectures, and electromagnetic optimization techniques.

Terahertz Devices

Modeling of plasma-wave devices and terahertz waveguide and amplifier systems.

Wireless Power Transfer

Innovative RF power telemetry, beam steering, and wearable energy harvesting systems.
